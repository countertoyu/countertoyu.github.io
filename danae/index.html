Danae

Acrisius, king of Argos, decides to consult the oracle about what his future looks like, but it can be very dangerous to try and know one's destiny.

"Be careful of your grand-son, who will take both your life and your throne", says the oracle.

At first he has no worries since he has no grand-son and his daughter is not married, but after some thought he starts to get worried and to be sure he decides to lock up his daughter in a brazen1 tower. Zeus does not appreciate the way the beautiful girl is treated and has a lot of pity for her, so he visits her in the form of a shower of gold and fathers Perseus.

Acrisius has thus just become a grand-father, and this situation was not without risks, as we will see later on.


1. brazen -> of brass; yellow metal.

Story source: "Greek Mythology, Prietenii Cărții, 1995", a collection coordinated by Wayne Leah and Cristina Ștefănescu.
